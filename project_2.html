Huang Lab

At the Huang Lab with Priniciple Investigator Professor Linfeng Huang and Postdoctorate Researcher Beibei Huo, we looked at siRNA (small interfering RNAs) in liver cancer cells. I first came into this lab as a Summer Research Scholar (SRS). We focused on the process of isolating and purifying siRNA from cells in where we induced their expression. Our group of five, would then use a nanodrop to measure the concentration of isolated siRNA. Professor Huang's Lab recently published a paper on the methods of inducing and isolating siRNA. Then, we would use reverse transfection to introduce these different siRNAs into eukaryotic cells. We would stain them with fluorescent dyes to analyze the toxicity of each strain of siRNA.
